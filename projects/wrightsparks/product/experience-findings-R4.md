# R4 Experience Findings — Service Discovery and Trust

Linked requirement: R4  
Mode: Experience Designer — Usability Review  
Status: accepted  
Date: 2026-07-12

## User problem

Potential customers must scan a long marketing page to establish both service fit and trust. In the pre-R4 rendered surface, six equally weighted service cards provide little help distinguishing home improvement, installation, compliance, and repair needs, while independent review evidence appears after several large sections.

## Affected user / workflow

Reading and Berkshire homeowners comparing electricians on desktop or mobile, especially users trying to answer “Do they handle my job?” before choosing a quote or phone action.

## Evidence

- The passing pre-R4 desktop capture shows a flat two-row service grid with no task-oriented grouping.
- Review evidence is positioned late in the page, after services, about, accreditations, service area, and work.
- Imported navigation and service content support service discovery and trust as core visitor needs.
- No behavioural analytics or direct user-interview evidence is available, so engagement improvement remains an assumption to validate after release.

## Assessment

- Confidence: high for the hierarchy issue; low for the projected engagement impact.
- Severity / frequency: medium-to-high; the service-fit decision is part of most prospective-customer visits.
- Finding type: clutter / hierarchy issue and usability issue.
- Recommendation type: UX improvement in scope.

## Recommendation and rationale

- Group all supported services into four plain-language categories rather than selecting an unsupported priority service.
- Give each service a consistent visual example, concise description, scan-friendly inclusions, and a direct quote anchor.
- Move source-verifiable review evidence directly after the opening trust band so trust is established before detailed service exploration.
- Preserve existing navigation, contact paths, page sections, brand palette, and frontend-only behavior.
- Do not invent review quotations, ratings, credentials, standards, or service claims that are absent from imported evidence.

This is a bounded information-hierarchy improvement, not a new feature or broad redesign. Recommended next roles: PM, then UI Designer, then Engineer.
