# Experience Designer Findings — R1

Mode: Pre-implementation usability review
Status: accepted
Requirement: R1

## Experience issue

People arriving with an electrical problem need rapid reassurance: what Wright Sparks handles, whether it serves homes or businesses, and how to ask for help. The current starter surface offers none of that decision support.

## Evidence and assumptions

- Evidence: R1 identifies unclear navigation, weak structure, and difficult service discovery in the existing experience.
- Evidence: the imported structure names Home, Services, About Us, Contact, testimonials, and project work as the core content.
- Assumption: contact is more urgent than online booking for this frontend-only slice; phone and email actions are therefore the primary resolution path.

## Usability requirements

- Answer service, audience, and contact questions in the hero without requiring exploration.
- Present one linear journey: orient, scan services, inspect proof, understand the company, contact.
- Use plain-language service categories and avoid unexplained trade terminology.
- Repeat contact access at the header, hero, final callout, and compact mobile action bar.
- Keep testimonial interaction optional: all critical information remains available without using it.
- Make focus, hover, selected, empty, loading, and error states legible.

## Scope routing

This is an in-scope experience improvement for R1. PM may task it for UI Designer and Engineer without a new feature requirement.
